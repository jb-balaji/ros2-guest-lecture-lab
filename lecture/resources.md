# Where to go from here — Resources

> Curated, short, and in the order you should actually use them. Each link has a one-line *when to read this*.

---

## Tonight (30–60 minutes)

- **Nav2 official docs — Getting Started** — https://docs.nav2.org/getting_started/index.html
  *When:* tonight. Walks you through exactly what we did, but on your own laptop if you want to install. Also explains every box on the architecture diagram from the lecture.
- **The Construct — TurtleBot3 free course** — https://app.theconstruct.ai/courses/ (filter for "TurtleBot3")
  *When:* tonight, if you'd rather stay in the browser. Picks up where the lecture left off.

## This weekend (2–4 hours)

- **Steve Macenski — "Navigation 2: The Story So Far"** (YouTube, ROSCon talks)
  *When:* when you want to hear the maintainer explain *why* Nav2 is built the way it is. Several talks; the most recent ROSCon talk is always the best one to start with.
- **ROS 2 Tutorials — beginner CLI tools** — https://docs.ros.org/en/jazzy/Tutorials.html
  *When:* if the `ros2 node`, `ros2 topic`, `ros2 run` commands still feel mysterious. 30 minutes well spent.
- **Foxglove Studio** — https://foxglove.dev/
  *When:* the moment you outgrow RViz. Modern, cross-platform, much easier to share visualizations.

## When you're ready to build your own thing

- **Nav2 Tuning Guide** — https://docs.nav2.org/tuning/index.html
  *When:* your robot kind of works but does dumb things. This is where you fix them.
- **SLAM Toolbox** — https://github.com/SteveMacenski/slam_toolbox
  *When:* you want to map your own environment with your own sensor data.
- **TurtleBot3 simulation repo** — https://github.com/ROBOTIS-GIT/turtlebot3_simulations
  *When:* you want to swap in a different world or modify the robot.

## If you want the math behind today's magic

- **Probabilistic Robotics** — Thrun, Burgard, Fox (MIT Press)
  *When:* you want to actually understand AMCL, SLAM, and Bayesian state estimation. Dense but the canonical book.
- **Stachniss SLAM lectures** (YouTube, University of Bonn)
  *When:* same content as Thrun, but spoken, with diagrams. Free. Excellent.

## Community

- **ROS Discourse** — https://discourse.ros.org/
  *When:* you have a real question and want a real answer. Surprisingly responsive.
- **Robotics Stack Exchange** — https://robotics.stackexchange.com/
  *When:* you have a specific debugging question. Good for "why does this error message mean what it means".
- **ROS Developers Podcast** (The Construct) — https://www.theconstruct.ai/category/podcast/
  *When:* commuting. Interviews with people building real robot products on ROS.
